Weekly outlook & analyst notes

The big picture: SPY has corrected 9.1% from the $697.84 ATH in exactly two months. The selloff has been orderly — no gap-down crash, no panic — but also relentless. Every bounce attempt (Mar 4, Mar 16-17, Mar 23-25) has been sold. This is the signature of institutional distribution: large holders unwinding positions methodically without triggering panic.

The Fibonacci story: The 23.6% retracement ($646.85) from the full Apr '25-Jan '26 swing was THE level to hold for bulls. It broke on Mar 26. This is significant because the 23.6% is the "first meaningful test" of a trend — if it fails, the market is telling you the trend has changed. The next Fibonacci support at 38.2% ($615.31) is 3% below current price. Between here and there, the pivot structure (S2 at $638, S3 at $631) provides intermediate support.

What to watch this week:

1. Can SPY hold $631-638 (S2-S3 zone)? Two tests of ~$644-645 have held so far. A third test often breaks.
2. VIX trajectory — currently 31. A spike to 35+ would be a contrarian buy signal for a short-term bounce. A gradual drift from 31 to 25 is more dangerous (complacency into continued selling).
3. The 200 DMA at $661.44 — this is the reclaim target. Until SPY gets back above it, every bounce is a sell-the-rip in a downtrend.
4. Bonds vs gold — if GLD continues outperforming TLT, the macro picture is more concerning than a simple equity correction.

Bottom line: Bearish bias maintained. The trend is down, every MA is overhead resistance, the Fib structure is broken, and momentum (RSI, MACD, ADX) all confirm sellers control the tape. Bounces should be treated as selling opportunities until SPY reclaims the 200 DMA. The high-probability trade is shorting rejections at $641-648 with targets at $631 and $615. For longs, wait for either a VIX spike above 35 or a daily close back above $661 — don't try to catch this falling knife on hope.
